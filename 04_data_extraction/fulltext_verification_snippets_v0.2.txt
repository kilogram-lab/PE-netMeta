28: Background—In patients with acute pulmonary embolism, systemic thrombolysis improves right ventricular (RV) dilatation, 
29: is associated with major bleeding, and is withheld in many patients at risk. This multicenter randomized, controlled trial 
30: investigated whether ultrasound-assisted catheter-directed thrombolysis (USAT) is superior to anticoagulation alone in 
31: the reversal of RV dilatation in intermediate-risk patients.
32: Methods and Results—Fifty-nine patients (63±14 years) with acute main or lower lobe pulmonary embolism and 
33: echocardiographic RV to left ventricular dimension (RV/LV) ratio ≥1.0 were randomized to receive unfractionated heparin 
34: and an USAT regimen of 10 to 20 mg recombinant tissue plasminogen activator over 15 hours (n=30; USAT group) 
35: or unfractionated heparin alone (n=29; heparin group). Primary outcome was the difference in the RV/LV ratio from 
36: baseline to 24 hours. Safety outcomes included death, major and minor bleeding, and recurrent venous thromboembolism 
37: at 90 days. In the USAT group, the mean RV/LV ratio was reduced from 1.28±0.19 at baseline to 0.99±0.17 at 24 hours

39: decrease in RV/LV ratio from baseline to 24 hours was 0.30±0.20 versus 0.03±0.16 (P<0.001), respectively. At 90 days, 
40: there was 1 death (in the heparin group), no major bleeding, 4 minor bleeding episodes (3 in the USAT group and 1 in the 
41: heparin group; P=0.61), and no recurrent venous thromboembolism.
42: Conclusions—In patients with pulmonary embolism at intermediate risk, a standardized USAT regimen was superior to 
43: anticoagulation with heparin alone in reversing RV dilatation at 24 hours, without an increase in bleeding complications.

73: Background: Patients with intermediate-high risk pulmonary embolism (PE) have an elevated 
74: right-to-left-ventricular (RV/LV) diameter ratio and are at risk of early clinical decompensation

77: test the efficacy and evaluate the safety of mechanical thrombectomy (MT), specifically 
78: computer assisted vacuum thrombectomy (CAVT) with anticoagulation (AC) compared to AC 
79: alone.  
80: Methods: STORM-PE is an international RCT with 1:1 randomization to CAVT with AC or AC 
81: alone. Eligible adults had acute onset (symptoms ≤14 days), intermediate-high risk PE, and were

85: core laboratory. Secondary endpoints included major adverse events (MAE) within 7 days (a 
86: composite of clinical deterioration necessitating rescue therapy, PE-related mortality,
